She loves to blur the boundaries between analogue & digital, and trying to find the greatest freedom & fun within the limits given in a virtual space.

Open to commissions and collaborations. Currently based in the Hague, the Netherlands.

A Room of Our Own
(Website, 2021)

What are the limits of the entities we call ‘family’ and ‘home’? Why are traditional notions of having our own space and family so important? A Room of Our Own invites guests into a virtual queer flat shared amongst six queer Koreans living in The Hague. The project engages alternative family formations within queer Korean diaspora living in the Netherlands, and the states of being ‘a stranger’ and ‘a wanderer’ in both countries. In this virtual house, the co-tenants who left their families of origin, create a space for self-definition, stability and comfort through shared living. It is an exploration of self-made spaces and self-made families and how they function away from the societies of their past.

Book as a Fossil
(Book, 2018)

Book as a Fossil is a compilation of visual elements from 10 different books.
It was established out of a need to show that books in our society became something of a shape of a fossil. It preserves not only content; so thoughts, feelings and ideas of an author, but also graphic style and aesthetics of the moment it was written in. In this book, we tried to show books as a fossil.

Made in collaboration with Anastasia Umpeleva, Agata Markowski and Martin Menso.
