Have you ever felt like a 'family' with Korean friends in the Netherlands? Is there any episode related?

It is comforting to share and sympathize with my hardships and episodes that I thought were really a problem. There's comfort that only those who have been through can do. They can understand my story ‘properly’ and I don’t have to explain everything from A to Z. That's when I feel like we are a family. In the past I didn't have that many Korean friends, so I had few people to talk to and I didn't want to tell anyone about my own problems or concerns. But this year, and also last year, we've been drinking and spending time together at your place. Not only your place but also my place, we've talked a lot about this and that. I think those times made me feel like this. I think there are many things that I learn by listening to various opinions and experiences of other people like me.
Do you feel that your life as a queer has changed since you came to the Netherlands? What is the difference between life as a queer in Korea?

In Korea, there are extremely few people who feel that I can come out and I'm less nervous about coming out here.
